## Device stack
Specify substrate, contacts, absorber, passivation, transport layers, interconnect, TCO, electrode, and encapsulation.

## Process flow
List main fabrication steps and critical parameters.

## Characterization
Use as relevant: JV, stabilized MPP, EQE, PL/TRPL, EL, XRD, GIWAXS, SEM/AFM, XPS/UPS/KPFM, ToF-SIMS, impedance, contact resistivity, optical transmittance/reflection.

## Stability test
Define light, temperature, humidity, atmosphere, encapsulation, MPP/open circuit, duration, sample count, and failure metric.

## Success criteria
Define measurable targets. Examples: PCE, Voc deficit, FF, EQE current match, T80, area uniformity, yield.

## Failure criteria
Define what would stop or redirect the route.

## Fallback plan
Give alternatives if the main route fails.

## Common validation modules

### Wide-bandgap top-cell stability
- Test halide segregation by PL/EL under illumination.
- Track MPP under relevant light and temperature.
- Compare passivation/contact strategies.

### Sn-Pb bottom-cell oxidation control
- Compare reducing additives, processing atmosphere, and encapsulation.
- Track Voc, EQE, PL, and shelf/operational stability.

### Transparent electrode damage
- Compare sputtering buffers, TCO conditions, sheet resistance, transmittance, and device performance.

### Textured silicon coverage
- Use SEM/cross-section, optical reflection, JV/EQE, and uniformity mapping.

### Mini-module scale-up
- Track coating uniformity, scribing loss, geometric fill factor, yield, and encapsulated stability.
