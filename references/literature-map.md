# Literature Map

This reference classifies perovskite and tandem solar cell research. Use it to understand where a question or paper belongs. It is not a current efficiency-record database.

## 1. Single-junction perovskite solar cells

Focus: high-efficiency devices, passivation, interfacial recombination, ion migration, hysteresis, stability, lead management, and scalable fabrication.

Key checks:
- stabilized PCE rather than only scan PCE
- hysteresis and scan direction
- MPP tracking
- EQE-integrated current
- device area and statistics
- whether the result transfers to wide-bandgap tandem top cells

R&D relevance: useful as a materials and interface platform, but single-junction results should not be assumed to translate directly to tandem devices.

## 2. Perovskite/silicon 2T monolithic tandem

Focus: wide-bandgap perovskite top cell, crystalline silicon bottom cell, current matching, recombination layer, transparent electrode, parasitic absorption, textured silicon compatibility, outdoor energy yield, and module integration.

Key checks:
- top-cell bandgap, commonly around the wide-bandgap range required for silicon tandems
- fully textured vs planar silicon
- conformal coverage on textured substrates
- low-temperature compatibility
- transparent-electrode sputtering damage
- recombination/interconnection losses
- certified vs self-reported efficiency
- cell vs module area

R&D relevance: often the most commercially relevant high-efficiency tandem route, but integration and reliability risks are high.

## 3. Perovskite/silicon 4T tandem

Focus: semitransparent perovskite top cell, independent silicon bottom cell, NIR transmittance, transparent electrodes, optical coupling, and module/system complexity.

Key checks:
- top-cell NIR transmission
- total 4T efficiency and separate subcell efficiencies
- optical losses from electrodes and encapsulation
- added system cost and wiring complexity

R&D relevance: good for rapid proof-of-concept and semitransparent applications; less obviously the default mainstream module route.

## 4. All-perovskite tandem

Focus: wide-bandgap Pb-based perovskite top cell, narrow-bandgap Sn-Pb bottom cell, Sn oxidation, voltage deficit, interconnection layers, flexible/lightweight applications, and low-temperature fabrication.

Key checks:
- top and bottom bandgaps
- Sn2+ oxidation control
- Voc deficit
- subcell-resolved current and voltage losses
- long-term operational stability
- flexible/module evidence

R&D relevance: attractive for lightweight, flexible, BIPV, vehicle-integrated, and high power-to-weight applications; reliability remains a central risk.

## 5. Perovskite/CIGS tandem

Focus: thin-film bottom cells, semitransparent perovskite top cells, flexible substrates, low-temperature compatibility, and thin-film module integration.

Key checks:
- CIGS bottom-cell quality
- top-cell NIR transmittance
- transparent electrode loss
- mechanical flexibility
- module integration path

R&D relevance: specialized thin-film and flexible opportunity; ecosystem is smaller than silicon.

## 6. Wide-bandgap perovskite top cells

Focus: mixed-halide perovskites, Br-rich compositions, halide segregation, Voc loss, interfacial recombination, SAM-based contacts, transparency, and light stability.

Key checks:
- bandgap and Voc deficit
- photoinduced phase segregation evidence
- PL/EL/QFLS analysis
- MPP stability under illumination
- transferability to tandem stacks

## 7. Sn-Pb narrow-bandgap bottom cells

Focus: Sn oxidation, self-doping, low Voc, carrier lifetime, reducing additives, interface energetics, infrared EQE, and operational stability.

Key checks:
- bandgap around low-bandgap tandem target range
- Voc deficit
- Sn oxidation strategy
- air stability and processing tolerance
- IR response

## 8. Manufacturing and modules

Focus: slot-die coating, blade coating, vapor deposition, co-evaporation, hybrid deposition, drying/crystallization, module scribing, geometric fill factor, encapsulation, yield, throughput, and cost.

Key checks:
- aperture/module area
- uniformity
- coating speed
- process window
- yield/statistics
- encapsulated stability
- equipment compatibility

## 9. Stability and reliability

Focus: ISOS-style testing, operational MPP stability, damp heat, thermal cycling, UV, light soaking, outdoor testing, encapsulation, lead leakage, and failure analysis.

Key checks:
- sealed vs unsealed
- MPP vs open circuit/storage
- temperature, humidity, light intensity
- test duration
- T80/T90 or remaining PCE
- continuous vs interrupted testing
- sample count

## 10. Techno-economic and life-cycle analysis

Focus: cost of ownership, capex, throughput, yield, module lifetime, balance-of-system impact, energy payback, carbon footprint, and end-of-life management.

Key checks:
- lifetime assumptions
- yield assumptions
- incremental process cost
- certified module performance
- degradation rate
- encapsulation and warranty assumptions
