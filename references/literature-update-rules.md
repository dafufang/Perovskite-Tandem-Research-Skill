# Literature Update and Search Rules

## Must verify with current sources

Search the web when the user asks about:
- latest, recent, current, this year, today, now, 2025/2026, or similar time-sensitive terms
- certified efficiency records
- NREL chart status
- company announcements, production, partnerships, financing, shipments, or commercialization
- specific DOI, title, author list, date, or journal details not supplied by the user
- policy, market, price, supply chain, or standards changes
- internal R&D decisions that depend on current competitive landscape

## Source priority

Use `source-priority.md`. Prefer primary or authoritative sources. For records, prefer NREL, certification labs, official institution releases, and peer-reviewed papers. Treat company claims as claims unless third-party certified.

## Screening standards

High-priority papers usually include one or more of:
- certified efficiency
- stabilized PCE
- MPP tracking
- EQE-integrated current
- clear device area
- statistics across multiple devices
- stability under meaningful stress
- scalable manufacturing method
- module or minimodule data
- diagnostics explaining mechanism

Deprioritize papers that rely only on:
- scan PCE without stabilized output
- missing area
- no statistics
- no stability data
- no EQE verification
- highly exotic materials or narrow process windows
- purely self-reported commercial claims

## Required extraction fields for papers

Extract:
- title, authors, year, journal, DOI if available
- route and device type
- top/bottom cell absorber, bandgap, contacts, interconnect, TCO, substrate, encapsulation
- PCE, stabilized PCE, certified PCE, Voc, Jsc, FF, area, EQE current
- stability conditions: light, temperature, humidity, encapsulation, MPP, duration, T80/T90
- process: spin, blade, slot-die, vapor, co-evaporation, hybrid, temperature, area, throughput
- scale-up relevance, reproducibility, risks, and recommended validation experiments

## Citation and uncertainty rules

- Cite current facts and specific records.
- Mark company data as official claim unless independently certified.
- Mark preprints as preprints and verify publication status if needed.
- If sources conflict, prefer certification bodies, NREL, peer-reviewed literature, or official institution releases over secondary media.
- If a fact cannot be confirmed, say it cannot be confirmed rather than guessing.
