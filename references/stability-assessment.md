# Stability Assessment

## Required questions

For every stability claim, extract:
- Encapsulated or unencapsulated?
- MPP tracking, open circuit, short circuit, storage, or periodic JV?
- Light intensity and spectrum?
- Temperature?
- Humidity?
- Atmosphere?
- Duration?
- Continuous or interrupted test?
- T80/T90 or remaining PCE?
- Device area and sample count?
- Indoor or outdoor?
- Cell, minimodule, or module?
- Any ISOS or IEC alignment?

## Evidence levels

### Strong evidence
- Encapsulated or clearly relevant device configuration.
- Continuous MPP tracking under meaningful light/temperature.
- Long duration with multiple devices.
- Clear area, statistics, and degradation metric.
- Ideally includes outdoor, damp heat, thermal cycling, or IEC-relevant stress.

### Medium evidence
- Meaningful stress test but missing one or more: encapsulation, long duration, statistics, or MPP.

### Weak evidence
- Short test, storage-only, unclear conditions, small sample count, no MPP, or no area/statistics.

### Only a lead
- Anecdotal stability, dark storage, unverified claim, unclear conditions, or company marketing claim without technical details.

## Common traps
- "Maintained 95%" without stating duration and conditions.
- Storage stability reported as operational stability.
- Unencapsulated short-term tests used to imply module reliability.
- Champion cell stability without statistics.
- Stable JV scan but no MPP tracking.
